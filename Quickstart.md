# Quickstart (5 minutes)

*The fastest path into Orchestrator OS. Read this, then go deeper with [the shortform guide](./the-shortform-guide.md) (30 min) or the full [longform guide](./the-longform-guide.md). Terms you do not recognize are in the [Glossary](./Glossary.md). ← [Orchestrator OS](./00_MOC.md).*

## What this is, in one sentence
An operating system for building with AI by **orchestration**: you run a persistent point-man for a domain who directs disposable sub-agents, instead of doing the leaf work yourself.

## The one rule
**Fan out for intelligence; keep writes single-threaded.** Many agents read, research, and critique in parallel. One integrator (you, or the orchestrator) makes the actual changes. The irreversible step always returns to a human.

## The five layers (the whole system)
1. **The Standard**: the mold, what an orchestrator must have to be born complete.
2. **The Ceremonies**: the operating sequences (factory mints one, gatekeeper enforces the mold, the master ceremony runs a task).
3. **The Orchestrators**: the persistent point-men, each with a builder doer-tier.
4. **The Building Blocks**: agents, commands, skills, and hooks (the hooks enforce the deterministic rules so they are not left to memory).
5. **The Sandbox**: the harness that proves a role stays in-role.

## Do this first
1. **Read the [philosophy](./the-philosophy.md)** (5 min) to get why orchestration beats a pile of prompts.
2. **Skim [the example orchestrator](./examples/00_EXAMPLES_INDEX.md)** to see the shape of a complete one.
